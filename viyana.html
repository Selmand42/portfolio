🇦🇹 Vienna Travel Plan

Vienna — 3 Days / Neubau Base

✈️ Arrival: 22 May 2026 (Friday) 10:00 — Vienna International Airport (VIE) 🏨 Stay: Myrthengasse 7, 07. Neubau, 1070 Vienna 🚇 Transport: Airport train/bus, metro, tram, walking 🎫 Tickets: Timed tickets recommended for Schönbrunn, Belvedere, Sisi Museum/Hofburg and KHM
Day 1 — 22 May (Friday) Arrival, Innere Stadt, St. Stephen’s Cathedral, Hofburg exterior, Ringstrasse, State Opera
Day 2 — 23 May (Saturday) Schönbrunn Palace, Naschmarkt, Kunsthistorisches Museum, Prater evening
Day 3 — 24 May (Sunday) Belvedere, Karlskirche, Hofburg/Sisi Museum, classic café, relaxed final dinner

📍 Base & Distance Notes — From Myrthengasse 7

Walkable core: MuseumsQuartier/KHM ~10–15 min walk, Rathaus ~15–20 min, Hofburg ~20 min, State Opera ~20–25 min.
By transit: St. Stephen’s Cathedral ~15–20 min, Schönbrunn ~25–35 min, Belvedere ~25–30 min, Prater ~25–35 min.
Evening returns: most central dinner areas are ~15–25 minutes from Myrthengasse by walking, tram or U-Bahn.

🎯 Vienna Must-Sees Included

This plan explicitly includes all 9 requested places: Schönbrunn Palace, St. Stephen’s Cathedral, Hofburg Palace / Sisi Museum, Belvedere Palace, Vienna State Opera, Kunsthistorisches Museum, Prater + Giant Ferris Wheel, Naschmarkt, and Rathaus + Ringstrasse.

DAY 1 — 22 MAY 2026 (FRIDAY) — ARRIVAL & OLD VIENNA
10:00
Arrive at Vienna International Airport (VIE)
Morning arrival gives you a strong first sightseeing day without overloading it
10:45–12:00
Airport → Myrthengasse / Neubau area
🚇 CAT/S7/REX to the city + U-Bahn/tram toward Volkstheater/Neubau; total usually ~45–60 min depending on connection
12:00–13:00
Lunch near Neubau / MuseumsQuartier
Keeps the first block practical after the flight and starts close to your base
13:30–15:00
St. Stephen’s Cathedral + Stephansplatz
Vienna’s symbolic center; the cathedral, towers and square are the natural first stop in the old city
🚇 Myrthengasse → Stephansplatz: ~15–20 min by U-Bahn/tram, or ~30 min walk
⚠️ English cathedral tours usually require checking the day’s schedule
15:00–16:30
Graben → Kohlmarkt → Hofburg Palace courtyards
Elegant imperial Vienna in one walk: cafés, historic streets and the Habsburg palace complex
16:45–18:15
Vienna State Opera exterior + Ringstrasse walk to Rathaus
The Opera anchors Vienna’s music identity; Ringstrasse and Rathaus show the city’s grand 19th-century architecture
🚶 Opera → Rathaus: ~20–25 min walk via Ringstrasse, or tram 1/2 for a partial loop
18:30–20:00
Dinner in the Innere Stadt or near the Opera
20:00–21:30
Optional: Vienna State Opera guided tour or standing-room performance check
A classic Vienna experience if timing and tickets work; otherwise the exterior and Ring walk already cover the landmark
🚇 State Opera → Myrthengasse: ~15–20 min by tram/U-Bahn, or ~20–25 min walk
DAY 2 — 23 MAY 2026 (SATURDAY) — SCHÖNBRUNN, ART & PRATER
09:00–12:30
Schönbrunn Palace + gardens + Gloriette view
The essential imperial summer palace; the gardens and Gloriette give the visit a strong sense of scale
⚠️ Palace is open daily; book a timed palace tour in advance
🚇 Myrthengasse → Schönbrunn: ~25–35 min by tram/U-Bahn; plan extra time for the gardens
13:00–14:15
Naschmarkt lunch
Saturday is the best fit: market stalls are active and it connects well with the museum quarter
🚇 Schönbrunn → Naschmarkt/Kettenbrückengasse: ~20–25 min
14:30–17:00
Kunsthistorisches Museum (KHM)
Vienna’s major art-history museum; a natural counterpart to the imperial palace day
⚠️ Book ahead if you want a specific slot; allow at least 2–2.5 hours
🚶 Naschmarkt → KHM: ~15–20 min walk; KHM → Myrthengasse: ~10–15 min walk
17:15–18:30
MuseumsQuartier / Maria-Theresien-Platz break
Good decompression after KHM; cafés and open courtyards nearby
19:00–21:30
Prater evening + Giant Ferris Wheel (Riesenrad)
A lighter, playful contrast to the imperial and museum-heavy day
🚇 Myrthengasse / MuseumsQuartier area → Praterstern: ~25–35 min by U-Bahn
DAY 3 — 24 MAY 2026 (SUNDAY) — BELVEDERE, HOFBURG & FINAL EVENING
09:00–11:00
Upper Belvedere
Home to Klimt’s “The Kiss” and one of Vienna’s most elegant Baroque palace settings
⚠️ Upper Belvedere is open daily 09:00–18:00; book the timed ticket early to avoid midday crowds
🚇 Myrthengasse → Upper Belvedere: ~25–30 min by tram/U-Bahn, or ~45 min walk
11:15–12:15
Karlskirche + Karlsplatz
One of Vienna’s most photogenic Baroque churches; easy walk from Belvedere
12:30–13:30
Lunch near Karlsplatz / city center
14:00–16:30
Hofburg Imperial Apartments + Sisi Museum
The best indoor look at Habsburg Vienna in the historic palace complex
⚠️ Open daily; last admission is usually 16:30, so do not push this later
🚶 Karlsplatz → Hofburg: ~15–20 min walk; Hofburg → Myrthengasse: ~20 min walk
16:45–18:00
Coffee break: Café Central / Demel / nearby classic café
Vienna café culture deserves a calm final stop
18:00–19:00
Golden-hour walk through Graben / Kohlmarkt or along the Ringstrasse
Keeps the final evening open and atmospheric instead of rushing straight back after the café stop
🚶 Hofburg / Café Central area → Ringstrasse or Graben: ~5–15 min walk
19:30–21:30
Final dinner in the Innere Stadt, Spittelberg or MuseumsQuartier area
A normal dinner window with enough time to enjoy the last night without making the day feel over-planned
🚶/🚇 City center → Myrthengasse: ~15–25 min depending on dinner location

🔄 Day 3 Alternatives

If you want a calmer last day: Skip the Sisi Museum and keep only Belvedere + Karlskirche + cafés.
If the weather is bad: Spend more time inside Belvedere or Hofburg and reduce outdoor walking around Karlsplatz.

📝 Practical Notes

  • Use the WienMobil or ÖBB app for real-time routing, especially for cross-city tram and U-Bahn connections.
  • Base address: Myrthengasse 7, 07. Neubau, 1070 Vienna; most central sights are walkable or 15–30 minutes by transit.
  • Schönbrunn Palace, Upper Belvedere and Sisi Museum/Hofburg are open daily in the checked official schedules.
  • Naschmarkt stalls are best Friday/Saturday; Sunday is not a market-stall day, so the plan uses Saturday lunch.
  • All 9 requested must-sees are included explicitly: Schönbrunn, Stephansdom, Hofburg, Belvedere, State Opera, KHM, Prater/Riesenrad, Naschmarkt, Rathaus/Ringstrasse.
